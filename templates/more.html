WANT TO KNOW MORE....

Here is the INFO

  1. POLYTECHNIC COURSES

    • A polytechnic course is a progressive learning experience that allows students to participate in a diploma or vocational course based on technical education.
    • Polytechnic offer hands-on practical education and training in technical fields such as engineering, computer sciences, and applied sciences.
    • If you are looking for junior-level jobs in an engineering field or want to start earning early, the polytechnic is a good course.
    • Polytechnic courses can be pursued after 10th or 12th.

  2. DIPLOMA COURSES

    • Diploma courses are an excellent choice as they allow transferable credits towards higher education such as a degree or master's program.
    • By preferring these courses students can work in the various fields of engineering such as civil, mechanical, electrical, computer science, electronics and communication, aerospace, and many more.
    • A diploma allows you to start sooner and work your way up.
    • Diploma is recognized as short-term courses focusing on vocational training and skill development.

  3. ITI COURSES

    • ITIs can provide students with the skills and knowledge needed to pursue a wide range of employment opportunities in various trades and occupations.
    • The full form of ITI is Industrial Training Institute and it is a government training organization responsible for providing high school students with industry-related education.
    • Many private sectors need skilled ITI professionals. So career options are wide in agriculture, textiles, and construction.
    • Various public sector units (PSUs) like railways, state-wise PWDs, BSNL, IOCL, ONGC and many more provide job opportunities for ITI students.
    • ITI students can also explore job opportunities in the Indian army, Indian navy, BSF, CRPF, and other paramilitary forces.

  4. VOCATIONAL COURSES

    • Vocational education or Vocational Education and Training (VET), also called Career and Technical Education (CTE), prepares learners for jobs that are based in manual or practical activities, traditionally non-academic and totally related to a specific trade, occupation.
    • Vocational education can be at the secondary or post-secondary level.
    • It is awarded to students who have completed degree requirements for a specific field or trade.
    • It can make students more attractive to potential employers. Many vocational education programs also offer internships and job placement assistance, helping students transition from education to employment.
